Privacy Policy

Sand Sort Lab

This policy explains how Sand Sort Lab handles information when you play the game. The current version does not require an account, and it uses Firebase services to collect app usage, diagnostics, and configuration data so we can improve game quality and player experience.

Last updated: June 3, 2026

Current data posture No account required. Firebase is used for analytics, diagnostics, and remote configuration.

No Account

You can play without creating an account or providing a name, email address, phone number, or login credential.

Firebase Analytics

We collect gameplay events and technical diagnostics to understand crashes, difficulty, performance, and feature usage.

On-Device Saves

Coins, lives, level progress, settings, and similar gameplay state are stored locally on your device.

Information We Collect

Sand Sort Lab does not require players to create an account. We do not ask for names, email addresses, phone numbers, precise location, photos, contacts, microphone access, camera access, or payment information in the current version.

Gameplay progress and preferences may be saved locally on your device so the game can remember your levels, coins, lives, sound settings, and similar in-game state.

The app also uses Firebase services, including Google Analytics for Firebase, Firebase Crashlytics, Firebase Remote Config, Firebase Installations, Firebase Sessions, and Firebase A/B Testing. Depending on device, configuration, and gameplay, these services may collect app usage, diagnostics, device, and installation data.

Analytics And Diagnostics

We use Firebase analytics events to understand how players use the game and where the experience can be improved. Events may include app opens, session duration, level start, level completion, level failure, restart, booster use, settings changes, and similar gameplay or UI interactions.

Firebase may also collect technical information such as app version, operating system, device model, approximate geography, language, time zone, installation identifiers, session information, and SDK performance metadata. Crashlytics may collect crash stack traces, relevant application state, device and OS information, and breadcrumb events before a crash. Remote Config may collect device country, language, time zone, OS version, app identifiers, and configuration metadata so we can safely adjust game parameters.

We do not intentionally send names, email addresses, phone numbers, precise location, or other direct contact information through Firebase events.

Third-Party Services

Firebase services are provided by Google. Firebase and Google Analytics may process data according to Google's terms and privacy practices. You can read more at Firebase Privacy and Security and Google Analytics for Firebase data collection.

The current version does not include advertising networks, social login, cloud save, or payment SDKs. If those services are added later, this policy and the App Store privacy disclosures should be updated before release.

How Information Is Used

Local gameplay data is used to provide app functionality, such as resuming progress, restoring game settings, displaying coin and life balances, and continuing unlocked levels.

Firebase analytics and diagnostics data is used to measure game balance, detect crashes, understand whether levels are too easy or too difficult, improve performance, test configuration changes, and prioritize bug fixes and product improvements.

Data Sharing

We do not sell player personal information. Firebase analytics and diagnostics data is shared with Google because Google provides the Firebase services used by the app. We do not use Firebase data to identify a player's real-world identity, and we do not share analytics data with advertising networks in the current version.

Data Retention And Deletion

Local game data remains on your device until you delete the app, clear the app data, or the app overwrites it during normal gameplay. Deleting the app removes locally stored game progress according to your device operating system behavior.

Firebase analytics and diagnostics data is retained according to the retention settings and controls in Firebase and Google Analytics. If you contact us through the App Store support channel with a privacy request, we will use the information you provide only to respond to that request and, where possible, help process deletion or access requests.

Children's Privacy

Sand Sort Lab is a general puzzle game and is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. The current analytics setup is intended for product quality and gameplay improvement. If a future version adds any feature that collects additional personal information, this policy will be updated and appropriate consent flows will be added where required.

Changes To This Policy

We may update this policy when the game changes. The latest version will be posted on this page with an updated date.

Contact

For privacy questions or deletion requests, contact the developer through the support contact listed on the App Store product page for Sand Sort Lab. If you voluntarily provide contact information when making a request, it will be used only to respond to that request.
